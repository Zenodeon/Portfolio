Kinetic Motion Table – Interactive Shape Shifting Surface

Worked on the Kinetic Motion Table, a dynamic physical display made from a grid of custom built linear motors that raise and lower to create realtime, animated topographies. The table reacts to user interaction and also plays kinetic motion sequences that captivate both visually and sonically.

Led the development of the software system, integrating Azure Kinect depth sensing for gesture-based control. As users move their hands above the surface, the table responds with smooth, physical motion transforming input into dynamic 3D shapes.

An overhead projector adds an additional layer of immersion by mapping realtime visual content onto the surface. This enhances the kinetic movement with color, light, and contextual visuals from highlighting gameplay elements (like coloring snake food) to amplifying preset animations with ambient effects.

Also contributed to hardware development and troubleshooting, helping resolve mechanical and integration issues during the build process.

Key features:
- Real-time hand tracking with Azure Kinect for gesture control and kinectic feedback
- Interactive applications like a physical Snake game, with projected feedback and responsive surface movement
- Preset motion playback, designed to mesmerize through synchronized motion and light
- The sound of the motors adds an ambient, mechanical rhythm that deepens the sensory experience
- Overhead projection dynamically enhances each motor's motion with light, color, and content alignment

The Kinetic Motion Table merges gesture interaction, mechanical motion, and projected visuals to create a powerful, multisensory experience that feels alive evoking curiosity.

The 2nd short demo video showcases the table’s motion playback capabilities using the iconic black-and-white animation Bad Apple!!. As the animation plays, the motorized pins rise and fall to recreate the frames in physical form, while the projector overlays the matching visuals, resulting in a synchronized, animated sculpture. This demo highlights the system’s ability to translate complex motion and visual content into an engaging, real-world kinetic display.
